# RERA and Legal Stuff

RERA stands for Real Estate Regulatory Authority, came from the Real Estate Act 2016.
Any project bigger than 500 sq meters needs to be registered with RERA.

Builders have to put 70% of the project money in a separate escrow account so they can't misuse it.
If there are delays or defects, buyers can complain directly to RERA.

One important thing - only carpet area counts for pricing now, not super built-up area.
Every ad for a project must show the RERA registration number.

Non-compliance penalties can go up to 10% of the total project cost which is huge.
Each state runs its own RERA so you need to check your state's portal.
Always verify RERA registration before buying anything, its easy to check online.
