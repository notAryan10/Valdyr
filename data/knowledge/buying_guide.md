# Tips for Buying Property

Check title deed and ownership history first, this is the most important step.
Also verify land use permissions from the local municipal office.

Your EMI ideally shouldn't be more than 40% of your monthly income.
Don't forget extra costs - stamp duty is 5-7%, registration about 1%, and GST 5% for under construction.

Get at least two independent valuations done before finalizing.
Check if there's any court case or dispute on the property.

Properties close to schools, hospitals and metro stations tend to appreciate faster.
Good ventilation and parking space make a big difference in resale value.

Listed prices are usually inflated by 10-15% so always negotiate.
If you're a first time buyer look into tax benefits under Section 80C and 24(b).
