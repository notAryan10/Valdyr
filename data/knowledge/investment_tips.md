# Real Estate Investment

Location matters the most. Look at infrastructure development and future growth plans.
Properties near upcoming metro lines or highways can appreciate 20-30% faster than others.

Rental yields in Indian cities are around 2-4% per year which isn't great but the capital appreciation makes up for it.

Under construction properties are cheaper but you're taking a risk on delivery timelines.
Ready to move ones cost more but no construction headaches.

Joint ownership helps with both tax benefits and getting a bigger loan.
If you don't want to buy physical property, REITs are an option.

Always check builder track record - how many projects completed on time, any RERA complaints etc.
Gated communities usually hold their value better during downturns.
